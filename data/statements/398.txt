=== PROBLEM 398 ===
source: https://www.erdosproblems.com/398

FALSIFIABLE
 
 Open, but could be disproved with a finite counterexample.

 Are the only solutions to\[n!=x^2-1\]when $n=4,5,7$?
 #398: [ErGr80,p.77]

 number theory | 

 factorials

--- additional ---
The Brocard-Ramanujan conjecture. Erdős and Graham describe this as an old conjecture, and write it 'is almost certainly true but it is intractable at present'. 

Overholt [Ov93] has shown that this has only finitely many solutions assuming a weak form of the ABC conjecture.

There are no other solutions below $10^9$ (see the OEIS page).

Naciri [Na25] has proved that there are only finitely many solutions if $x\pm 1$ is either $k$-free (for some $k\geq 2$) or a prime power (and Cambie explains both facts in the comments), and that if $x\pm 1$ is $7$-free then $n=4,5,7$ give the only solutions.
